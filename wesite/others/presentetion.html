Presentation

Create an easy-to-follow structure

  • A compelling introduction:Your introduction needs to briefly sum up what you’re going to talk about
  • Offer a body of evidence:you should not stay stiff in one place like a statue but to move and I don't meen breakdance, but your body language
  • No more than 10 slides in total:Who wants to sit through pages and pages of slides? No one, that’s who.

Be savvy with design details

  • Use color sparinglyBright colors can dazzle, but too many can be offputting. Use the colors most relevant to your message.
  • Be consistent with your fontConsistent design makes you look more professional
  • Format for perfectionIf it does not look very good it will look like you haven’t tried very hard (or worse, that you just aren’t very good)

Be genuine

  • Use humor.To break the ice
  • Don’t be afraid to mess uphumans learn from failures and become better
